Sambalpuri Saree

Berhampur Phoda Kumbha in contrasting contemporary borders. Introduced at Berhampur town in the 14th century AD by the Mohuri Kings. The Berhampur phoda Kumbha has a unique pattern of weaving, particularly the designs in the contemporary temple borders. Woven in Berhampur, “The Silk City of Odisha”, the Berhampuri Patta is unique for its thick silk, which has a special texture.

Berhampuri Silk Patta

A Sambalpuri saree is a traditional handwoven saree from Odisha, India, renowned for its unique patterns and exquisite craftsmanship. Made using the “Bandha” tie-dye technique, similar to Ikat, the designs are dyed onto the yarn before weaving, resulting in intricate and precise patterns. These sarees often showcase nature-inspired motifs like flowers, wheels, and conch shells, along with geometric designs. Crafted from cotton or silk, they are versatile for both casual and formal wear. Known for their vibrant colors and fine artistry, Sambalpuri sarees are cherished both in India and globally.
